Classic Pot Roast

Ingredients

  • 3–4 lb beef chuck roast
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 3 celery stalks, cut into chunks
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour (optional, for thickening)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (160°C).
  2. Season the beef roast with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large oven-safe pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  4. Sear the roast on all sides until browned, then remove from the pot.
  5. Add onions, carrots, and celery to the pot; cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  6. Add garlic and tomato paste; cook for 1 minute more.
  7. Pour in beef broth and red wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.
  8. Return the roast to the pot. Add thyme and bay leaves.
  9. Cover and cook in the oven for 3–4 hours, or until the meat is tender and falls apart easily.
  10. Remove the roast and vegetables. (Optional) Thicken the remaining liquid by whisking in flour and simmering on the stove until thickened.
  11. Serve the roast with vegetables and gravy.
